Are Bybit and Hyperliquid competitors?

Bybit and Hyperliquid are direct competitors in the cryptocurrency derivatives market, specifically for perpetual futures. While they share similar target audiences of retail and professional traders, they represent two different business philosophies: Bybit is a centralized exchange (CEX), whereas Hyperliquid is a decentralized exchange (DEX) built on its own Layer 1 blockchain.

Core Business Comparison

  • Custody & Control: Bybit acts as a custodian, holding users' funds in its own internal asset storage systems. Hyperliquid is self-custodial, meaning users keep control of their private keys and assets are stored directly on-chain.
  • Identity Requirements: Bybit requires KYC verification for all users. Hyperliquid is permissionless and does not require identity verification, allowing for greater privacy.
  • Infrastructure: Bybit uses an off-chain order book and matching engine for high-speed execution. Hyperliquid utilizes an on-chain Central Limit Order Book (CLOB), which provides full transparency on all trades and liquidations.

Key Performance Metrics (2026)

  • Trading Volume: Bybit typically processes over $15 billion in 24-hour derivatives volume. Hyperliquid has rapidly grown to handle between $8 billion and $10 billion daily.
  • Fees: Hyperliquid's base taker fees (approximately 0.04% - 0.045%) are generally lower than Bybit's standard taker fees (0.055%).
  • Leverage: Bybit offers up to 100x leverage on major assets. Hyperliquid capped its leverage at 40x for Bitcoin and 25x for Ethereum following a significant $4 million loss incident in early 2025.

Revenue and Ecosystem

  • Bybit: Operates as a private company, offering a wide ecosystem including fiat-to-crypto gateways, staking (Bybit Earn), and even copy trading features.
  • Hyperliquid: Distributes a significant portion of its revenue back to the community; approximately 54% of revenue is used for buybacks of its native $HYPE token. It also offers "Vaults," which act as a decentralized form of copy trading.

Who are the CEOs?

The leaders for Bybit and Hyperliquid as of April 2026 are:

  • Bybit CEO: Ben Zhou. He is a co-founder of the company and has led its growth from its inception in 2018 into one of the world's largest cryptocurrency exchanges.
  • Hyperliquid Founder/CEO: Jeff Yan. A Harvard mathematics and computer science graduate, Yan co-founded Hyperliquid Labs after previously running a major anonymous market-making firm called Chameleon Trading.

Leadership Profiles

Ben Zhou (Bybit)

  • Background: Formerly worked in the traditional forex brokerage industry.
  • Strategic Focus: In 2026, he is focused on evolving Bybit into a "New Financial Platform" that integrates traditional banking services with cryptocurrency and explores AI-driven "Agentic Finance".
  • Recent Activity: Zhou recently appeared at Paris Blockchain Week 2026 to discuss the convergence of AI and programmable assets in the global financial infrastructure.

Jeff Yan (Hyperliquid)

  • Background: A former quant trader at Hudson River Trading. He built Hyperliquid with a lean team of just 11 core contributors and notably rejected all venture capital funding to maintain platform neutrality.
  • Strategic Focus: Yan views Hyperliquid as an "eternal store of value" and aims for it to become the "internet of money" by building decentralized, high-performance financial infrastructure.
  • Recent Activity: An in-depth profile by Colossus in April 2026 highlighted his success in capturing a significant share of the decentralized perpetuals market while maintaining a low public profile.
